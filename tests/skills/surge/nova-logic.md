# PRD Draft: Project "Nova-Logic" (Sci-Fi Creative Engine)

## 1. Project Objective

The goal is to architect and generate a multi-chapter Science Fiction novel that balances commercial viability with high-concept innovation. The system must autonomously determine the setting, core conflict, and unique technological/metaphysical hooks.

## 2. Core Requirements

### 2.1 Market Alignment

* **Popular Themes:** The content should align with current high-demand sci-fi sub-genres (e.g., Cyberpunk, Space Opera, Hard Sci-Fi, or Multiverse theory).
* **Commercial Appeal:** Must include elements that resonate with modern audiences (e.g., existential stakes, relatable protagonists, or social commentary).

### 2.2 Innovation & Uniqueness

* **Anti-Cliché Filter:** The Skill must intentionally avoid overused tropes (e.g., "AI taking over the world" or "Alien invasion") unless they are subverted in a fundamentally new way.
* **The "X-Factor":** Propose at least one "First-Principle" innovation—a scientific, social, or philosophical concept that has not been widely explored in mainstream media.

### 2.3 Structural Integrity

* **Chapter-Based Delivery:** The narrative must be divided into a logical, multi-chapter structure.
* **Pacing:** Each chapter must serve a specific narrative function (Exposition, Rising Action, Climax, etc.).

## 3. Deliverables

* **Master Concept:** A summary of the world-building, the unique "hook," and the central conflict.
* **Chapter Outline:** A breakdown of at least 5 chapters with titles and brief plot objectives.
* **Character Archetypes:** Brief descriptions of the key players driving the plot.
* **Language:** Chinese

## 4. Evaluation Criteria for the Skill

* **Autonomy:** Does the Skill ask for a specific sub-genre, or does it autonomously research and pick the most "viral-ready" one?
* **Creativity:** How "un-cliché" is the proposed core innovation?
* **Coherence:** Is the transition between chapters logical and self-consistent?
